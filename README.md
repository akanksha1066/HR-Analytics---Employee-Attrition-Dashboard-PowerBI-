#  HR Analytics - Employee Attrition Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This repository contains an interactive **HR Analytics Dashboard** developed in Power BI. The primary objective of this project is to analyze corporate workforce data, discover hidden patterns behind employee turnover (attrition), and provide data-driven insights to help HR managers improve employee retention.
## 🖥️ Dashboard Preview
![HR Attrition Dashboard](dashboard_screenshot.png)
## 📊 Key Features & Visualizations
The dashboard delivers a comprehensive view of workforce metrics through interactive sections:
* **Executive KPI Cards:** Quick metrics tracking **Total Employee Count**, **Average Employee Age**, and **Average Monthly Income**.
* **Department-wise Distribution:** A dynamic Pie Chart highlighting attrition and employee distribution across different departments (R&D, Sales, HR).
* **Age Demographic Trend:** A Line Chart analyzing the correlation between employee age and attrition rates to identify vulnerable age groups.
* **Compensation Analysis:** A Clustered Column Chart comparing the **Average Monthly Income** between employees who left versus those who stayed.
* **Interactive Data Filtering:** Integrated an **Attrition Slicer** allowing users to seamlessly filter the entire dashboard by 'Yes' or 'No' states for deep-dive analysis.

## 💡 Key Insights Captured
* **Income Correlation:** Employees who left the organization ('Yes' Attrition) had a noticeably lower average monthly income compared to those who stayed.
* **Age Factor:** Attrition peaks within specific younger age brackets, suggesting a need for better engagement strategies for freshers/early-career professionals.
* **Department Insights:** The Research & Development and Sales departments accounted for the highest volume of workforce distribution and turnover.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Skills Used
* **Business Intelligence Tool:** Power BI Desktop
* **Data Transformation:** Power Query (Data cleaning, handling data types)
* **Data Modeling:** Analytical field grouping and aggregation overrides (e.g., configuring Sum to Average metrics)
* **Data Visualization:** KPI Cards, Line Charts, Pie Charts, Bar Charts, and Interactive Slicers
